## Purpose

This GIF is the primary motion asset for the Legendary RPG landing page hero section and the Product Hunt gallery. It must communicate the full arc of the experience — from blank character to a living, breathing world — in under 45 seconds. It is the single asset most likely to convert a curious visitor into a waitlist signup.

**Priority moments** (in order of visual impact):
1. Portrait generation appearing from nothing — this is the hero moment; let it breathe
2. WorldThreshold cinematic overlay — first look at the world
3. GM narrative text streaming in — the product's signature feel
4. Dynamic scene art responding to the story

---

## Pre-Recording Setup

### Browser and window
- Browser: Chrome or Safari, latest version
- Window size: **1280 × 800** — set this exactly before recording
- Zoom level: 100% (default)
- Hide the browser toolbar/address bar if your recording tool supports it. If not, crop the address bar out in post
- Open an **Incognito / Private window** to avoid extensions, notifications, and any saved state cluttering the recording
- Sign in to a test account before starting the recorder — do not record the login process

### Account prep
- Have a test account already created and signed in at https://llm-game-master.replit.app
- The dashboard should be showing with no characters yet (or start from the "Create Character" flow directly)
- Confirm the app is responsive and fast before you start — do a trial run

### Recording tool
- Mac: **Cleanshot X** (recommended) or built-in **Screenshot.app** (Cmd+Shift+5 → Record Selected Portion)
- Set the capture region to exactly 1280 × 800 before starting
- Record at a high frame rate (30 fps) — you will convert to GIF after

### GIF conversion (after recording)
- Tool: **Gifski** (Mac app, free on App Store) — best quality-to-size ratio
- OR: Ezgif.com → Upload → Optimise → reduce to ≤5 MB
- Target: 15–20 fps in the final GIF (lower than source is fine; keeps file size down)
- Palette: 128–256 colours

## Scene-by-Scene Sequence

### Scene 1 — Character Creation: Name + Class (0:00–0:08)

**Start URL:** https://llm-game-master.replit.app (dashboard → "Create a New Character" button)

1. The recording begins on the character creation wizard, Step 1 (Name + Class)
2. Pause for 1 second so the viewer can read the screen
3. Click into the character name field
4. Type a compelling name — **type slowly, one character every ~0.3 seconds** so the typing is legible: suggest `Kael Dorryn` or `Sera Voss`
5. After the name is typed, pause for 0.5 seconds
6. Click on a class card — choose one with a visually interesting icon (Wizard or Ranger recommended for visual flair)
7. The class card should visibly highlight/select on click
8. Pause 0.5 seconds on the selected state — let the amber border glow register

**Pacing note:** This scene should feel deliberate and legible, not rushed. The viewer needs to understand what they are watching.

---

### Scene 2 — Portrait Generation (0:08–0:22)

**This is the hero moment. Give it the most time.**

1. Click the "Next" or "Generate Portrait" button to advance to the portrait step
2. The loading state begins — **do not speed this up**. The loading microcopy ("Painting your legend...", "The artist is at work...") is charming and should be visible for at least 2–3 seconds
3. Watch the portrait appear — the DALL-E 3 image loading in is visually stunning
4. Once the portrait is fully loaded, **linger for 3–4 seconds** — let the viewer absorb the quality of the image
5. Slowly move the mouse away from the portrait so nothing obscures it
6. Do not click anything for those 3–4 seconds — this image is the hook

**Pacing note:** This scene should be ~14 seconds total. The portrait reveal is the single most powerful moment in the GIF. Do not rush it. If the generation takes longer than expected, that is fine — the anticipation builds engagement.

---

### Scene 3 — Campaign Start (0:22–0:28)

1. Click "Next" or "Continue" to proceed through any remaining character creation steps
2. Navigate to campaign creation — click "Begin a New Chronicle" (or equivalent button)
3. Keep the campaign creation form interaction brief — the viewer does not need to read the form details
4. Fill in a campaign name if required (type quickly: `The Shattered Expanse`)
5. Click the "Begin" or "Start Campaign" CTA
6. The screen transitions to the Party Lobby or directly to the WorldThreshold overlay

**Pacing note:** Move through this scene at a confident, purposeful pace. 6 seconds total.

---

### Scene 4 — WorldThreshold Cinematic Overlay (0:28–0:33)

1. The WorldThreshold overlay appears — this is a full-screen cinematic moment
2. **Stop all mouse movement immediately** — let the overlay speak for itself
3. Hold on the WorldThreshold screen for **4–5 full seconds**
4. If the overlay has an animation (fade-in, text reveal), let it play completely before advancing

**Pacing note:** The WorldThreshold overlay is the "opening title card" of the campaign. Treat it like a cinema moment — no rushing, no mouse hovering. Pure stillness.

---

### Scene 5 — GM Narrative Streaming (0:33–0:42)

1. Click "Begin" or the enter-game CTA from the WorldThreshold overlay
2. The in-game Chronicle screen loads with the background scene art
3. The first GM message begins streaming in — **do not click anything while it streams**
4. Let the text stream completely, word by word, for the full effect
5. The dynamic background art behind the text is part of the visual — make sure it is visible (the text overlay card should not cover it entirely)
6. Once the GM message has streamed in fully, pause for 1–2 seconds

**Pacing note:** This is the second most important moment after the portrait. The streaming text animation is the product's signature. Let it run at full speed — do not try to scroll or interact. 9 seconds total.

---

### Scene 6 — Player Action + GM Response (0:42–0:48, trim to fit)

1. Click into the player message input at the bottom of the Chronicle
2. Type a short, evocative action — suggest: `I approach the ruined gate and listen` — type at normal speed
3. Click the gold send button (or press Enter)
4. The GM response begins streaming — let it start for 3–4 seconds (does not need to complete)
5. End the recording while the GM response is mid-stream or just completed

**Pacing note:** The GIF can end here, mid-stream. Ending on a cliffhanger ("what happens next?") is more engaging than a clean resolution. The viewer's curiosity should be the last frame.

## What to Avoid

- Do not show the browser address bar or browser UI (crop or hide it)
- Do not hover the mouse over important visual content — move the cursor to a neutral area during hold moments
- Do not click through loading states faster than they naturally resolve
- Do not include any error states, loading spinners that hang, or unexpected UI
- Do not record any personal account information, payment details, or email addresses in visible fields
- If anything goes wrong mid-recording, stop and restart — do not try to recover in the same take

The recording itself should take 20–30 minutes including a few takes. The first take is usually a rehearsal — use it to check timing, then record the final take clean.
