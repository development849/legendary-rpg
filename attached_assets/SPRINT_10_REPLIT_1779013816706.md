## Background

UXF-011 (filed 2026-05-04, P3): The world-consistency guardrail added in UXF-007 fires correctly — it deflects physically impossible actions. However, the GM's redirect tone is too casual and meta:

> *"A giant dragon? That's, uh, ambitious. But alas, transformation magic like that isn't exactly in the realm of possibility for you…"*

This breaks the cinematic, GM-authoritative voice established everywhere else in the game. The hedge reads as an LLM self-aware moment, not a Game Master maintaining world integrity.

**Target tone:** The GM should acknowledge the impossible impulse with in-world conviction, redirect to a plausible action, and continue — no meta-commentary, no "That's, uh", no breaking the fourth wall.

**Target example response:**
> *"Your body refuses the impossible — Kael Dawnstrider has no such power in this world. But instinct takes over and you lunge forward…"*
